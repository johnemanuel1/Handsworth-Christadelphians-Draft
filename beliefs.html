Our foundation

The Bible guides our faith

Christadelphians seek to base their beliefs and way of life on the Bible. We read both the Old and New Testaments, believing that together they reveal God's character, his purpose and his hope for humanity.

We do not expect anyone to accept something simply because we say it. We encourage thoughtful reading, honest questions and careful discussion of the Bible.

A simple overview

What we believe

These are some of the central beliefs that shape our faith.

01

One God

There is one God, the Father, who created all things and lovingly invites people to know him.

02

Jesus, God's Son

Jesus is the Son of God and the promised Messiah. Through his life, death and resurrection, forgiveness and hope are offered.

03

The Kingdom of God

Jesus will return to the earth to establish God's kingdom, bringing justice, peace and renewal.

04

Resurrection and hope

Our hope is resurrection from the dead and everlasting life in God's kingdom when Jesus returns.

05

Faith leads to repentance, baptism by immersion and a lifelong commitment to follow Jesus.

06

A life of love

Following Jesus means loving God and our neighbours, serving others and growing in faith together.

Our shared foundation

A concise summary of our beliefs

We believe the Bible is the inspired word of God and our authority for belief and conduct. It reveals one God, the Father, whose Holy Spirit is his power at work.

Jesus Christ is God's Son, conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of Mary. He shared our human nature, was tempted yet remained sinless, died for our sins and was raised to immortal life by God.

The gospel is the good news of God's Kingdom and the saving work of Jesus. Jesus will return personally to the earth, raise and judge the dead, and establish God's worldwide Kingdom.

Human beings are mortal, and death is an unconscious state. Eternal life is God's gift through Jesus, offered by grace to those who believe the gospel, repent, are baptised by full immersion and continue as his disciples.

Angels are God's immortal and obedient servants who do not sin. The Bible traces evil to sin and human desire, not to a supernatural rival to God.

For a fuller formal summary, read the Birmingham Amended Statement of Faith.

Belief in practice

How faith shapes our life

On Sundays we share bread and wine to remember Jesus, as he asked his followers to do. We also spend time learning from the Bible, praying and singing together.

Throughout the week, we try to live quietly and sincerely as followers of Jesus. This includes caring for one another, helping people in need, seeking peace and showing kindness.

We know that faith involves continuing to learn and grow. Our community is a place where people can support one another through the joys, questions and difficulties of life.
