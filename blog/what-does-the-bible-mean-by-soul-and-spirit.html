What Does the Bible Mean by Soul and Spirit?

Suggested reading: Genesis 2:7

Words such as soul and spirit often carry assumptions gathered from culture, philosophy and religious tradition. Reading how the Bible itself uses them can reveal a different picture.

Many people imagine a human being as a temporary body containing an immortal soul. Christadelphians believe the Bible generally describes the person as a living whole whose life depends upon God.

A living being

Genesis 2:7 provides the basic pattern. God forms the man from dust, breathes into him the breath of life, and the man becomes a living being. In older translations, the final phrase is "living soul". The man is not described as receiving a separate soul; he becomes a living soul.

The Hebrew word translated soul can refer to a person, a life or a living creature. It is used of animals as well as humans. Souls can be hungry, rescued, endangered and even die. Its Bible meaning is therefore broader and more concrete than an indestructible part that escapes death.

Breath, life and attitude

The words translated spirit can mean wind, breath or an unseen power. Human life continues because God gives the breath or spirit of life. At death, that life-giving power returns to God who gave it, while the person returns to the dust.

This does not mean a conscious personality travels to heaven. Ecclesiastes, which describes the spirit returning to God, also says the dead are no longer conscious of earthly activity. The Bible's hope is not that an immortal spirit naturally survives, but that God remembers and raises the person.

Spirit can also describe a person's attitude or disposition. Scripture speaks of a humble spirit, a troubled spirit or a spirit of gentleness. In these contexts, the word describes the inner direction of a person's thoughts and character.

Reading words in context

No important Bible teaching should depend only on one English word. Translation is necessary because Scripture was written mainly in Hebrew and Greek, and the same original word may have several related meanings. Context shows whether soul means a person or life, and whether spirit means breath, attitude or God's power.

This careful reading protects the Bible's central hope. Human beings are mortal and completely dependent upon God, yet death is not beyond His power. God can restore the whole person to life through resurrection.

Useful reading questions

  • Does soul refer here to a person, creature or life?
  • Does spirit mean breath, attitude or God's power?
  • What does the surrounding passage say about life and death?
  • Is the passage describing present life or future resurrection?

These words matter because they influence how we understand death, salvation and the future. The next article examines two more familiar terms, heaven and hell, and asks how they fit the Bible's wider message.
