What Does It Mean to Follow Jesus?

Suggested reading: Luke 9:18–25

The gospel invites people not only to believe facts about Jesus, but to become his disciples. A disciple is a learner who trusts a teacher enough to follow their way of life.

Following Jesus begins with grace. We do not earn God's love by becoming good enough. God calls imperfect people, offers forgiveness through Jesus and patiently shapes those who respond.

Learning from Jesus

Disciples listen to Jesus through the Gospels and the wider Bible. They learn what he teaches about God, the Kingdom, prayer, money, relationships, forgiveness and hope. This learning is intended to move from the page into life.

Jesus shows that the greatest commands are to love God wholeheartedly and love our neighbour. He welcomes overlooked people, confronts hypocrisy, forgives, serves and speaks truth with courage. Following him means allowing that pattern to challenge our instincts and choices.

Taking up the cross

Jesus told his disciples to deny themselves, take up their cross daily and follow him. This does not mean seeking suffering or ignoring healthy needs. It means surrendering the assumption that our own desires must always come first.

Christian discipleship includes costly choices: forgiving when resentment feels easier, telling the truth when dishonesty is convenient, serving without recognition and remaining faithful when hope feels distant. These choices do not purchase salvation. They are the fruit of trusting Jesus.

Growing with others

Jesus did not call isolated individuals only. He formed a community of disciples who had to learn patience, humility and love together. The early believers met to remember Jesus through bread and wine, learn from the apostles, pray and care for one another.

Christian community is not a gathering of people who have finished growing. It is a place where ordinary people encourage one another, repair relationships and continue learning.

Living toward the Kingdom

Disciples follow a risen Lord who will return. The coming Kingdom gives direction to present life. Believers seek to reflect its values now without imagining they can establish it by their own power.

Following Jesus includes

  • continuing to learn from his words and example;
  • praying and trusting God;
  • turning from sin and receiving forgiveness;
  • loving, serving and forgiving others;
  • meeting with fellow believers;
  • living in hope of his return and God's Kingdom.

Discipleship is a lifelong direction rather than a record of unbroken success. Followers of Jesus still fail and need grace. What changes is where they turn: back to God, back to Jesus' example and back to the hope of the gospel.

This pathway began by asking what the gospel is. It ends with an invitation. The good news of God's Kingdom and Jesus Christ is not merely a subject to understand. It is a hope to trust and a life to begin.
