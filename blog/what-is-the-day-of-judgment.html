What Is the Day of Judgment?

Suggested reading: Acts 17:22–31

The idea of judgment can feel uncomfortable, especially when it has been used to frighten or control people. Yet a world without judgment would also be a world where injustice never receives an answer.

The Bible presents judgment as part of the good news that God will put the world right through Jesus. It is serious because human choices matter, and hopeful because the judge is righteous, merciful and fully informed.

A day appointed by God

In Athens, Paul announced that God had appointed a day to judge the world in righteousness through the man He raised from the dead. The resurrection establishes Jesus as judge and gives confidence that God's promised future will arrive.

Judgment is connected with Jesus' return and the resurrection. Those made responsible through knowing God's call will answer for their response. Jesus will reveal what is true, recognise faithful service and establish God's righteous rule.

Perfect justice and mercy

Human judgment is limited. We misunderstand motives, overlook hidden suffering and sometimes favour the powerful. God knows every circumstance and judges without corruption. Jesus understands human weakness because he experienced temptation and suffering himself.

The Bible does not present salvation as a reward for flawless performance. Nobody could stand on that basis. Those accepted receive life by God's grace through Jesus. At the same time, genuine faith becomes visible in how people respond to mercy, treat others and use what they have been given.

Jesus' parables frequently join readiness with ordinary faithfulness: caring for people, using opportunities well and continuing to trust while the master seems delayed. Judgment reveals whether faith has become real in life.

What happens after judgment?

Those accepted by Jesus receive immortality and share in God's Kingdom. Those rejected do not suffer eternally; they face shame, loss and final death. This makes the gift of life precious while remaining consistent with God's justice and character.

Judgment also extends beyond individuals. Jesus will confront oppressive powers, end war and establish justice among nations. The Day of Judgment begins the public setting-right of a world damaged by sin.

Bible judgment means

  • God will answer injustice rather than ignore it;
  • Jesus is the appointed and compassionate judge;
  • knowledge and opportunity bring responsibility;
  • salvation remains a gift of grace;
  • faith is revealed through the lives people choose to live.

The right response is neither panic nor presumption. It is to trust God's mercy, take Jesus seriously and use the present as an opportunity to grow in faith and love.

Christian communities regularly remember that mercy and responsibility meet in Jesus. The final article explores why believers break bread together in remembrance of him.
