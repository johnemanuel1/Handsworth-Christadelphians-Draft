What Is the Kingdom of God?

Suggested reading: Isaiah 11

The Kingdom of God is the Bible's promise that God will rule the world through Jesus Christ, bringing lasting justice, peace and renewal to the earth.

It was at the heart of Jesus' message. He announced its good news, taught people to pray for it and told his followers to seek it first.

A Kingdom on earth

Many people hear "Kingdom of God" and think immediately of going to heaven. Yet Jesus taught his followers to pray, "Your kingdom come. Your will be done on earth as it is in heaven."

The Bible's hope is not that God abandons His creation, but that He restores it. The prophets describe nations learning peace, rulers acting justly, suffering being removed and the knowledge of God filling the earth.

Jesus as the promised king

God promised David a descendant whose kingdom would endure. Jesus is that promised king. His resurrection confirms that he lives, while his return will bring his reign openly to the world.

Isaiah 11 describes this ruler judging with righteousness rather than appearances. He protects the poor, confronts wickedness and brings peace. His leadership is unlike the flawed rule people repeatedly experience because he perfectly reflects God's character.

More than a political system

The Kingdom includes government, justice and transformed nations, but it is more than a better political arrangement. It is a world shaped by God's values and filled with people who know Him.

It also brings the Bible's earlier promises together. Abraham was promised an inheritance and blessing for all nations; David was promised an enduring throne. In God's Kingdom, those promises find their fulfilment through Jesus and include everyone who belongs to him.

Sin, suffering and death will ultimately be overcome. Those granted eternal life will share in the renewed creation. The Bible closes with God dwelling with humanity and wiping away tears.

God's Kingdom will bring

  • Jesus ruling as God's appointed king;
  • justice for the vulnerable and oppressed;
  • peace among nations;
  • resurrection and eternal life;
  • the renewal of the earth and true knowledge of God.

A future hope that shapes today

Christians cannot establish God's Kingdom through human power. It comes through God's action and the return of Jesus. Yet those who believe this future are called to reflect its values now.

Jesus teaches mercy, integrity, peacemaking, generosity and trust in God. Living these values does not create the Kingdom, but it shows that we genuinely desire God's rule.

The Kingdom offers hope larger than personal survival. It answers the longing for a world put right. It means history is not moving endlessly through cycles of conflict and loss, but toward the purpose God promised.

That purpose depends upon the return of its king. The next article considers the Bible's promise that Jesus will come back to the earth.
