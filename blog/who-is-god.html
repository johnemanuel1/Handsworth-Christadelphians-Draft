Who Is God?

Suggested reading: Exodus 34:4–8

The gospel begins with God. Before asking what God can do for us, the Bible invites us to discover who He is, what He is like and what purpose He is carrying forward.

The Bible consistently presents one God, the Father, who created all things. He is not part of the universe or limited by it. Life, meaning and hope begin with Him.

The Creator who remains involved

Genesis introduces God as the source of a world that is ordered, purposeful and good. Human beings are created in His image, given dignity and invited to reflect His character. Although humanity turns away, God does not abandon His creation. The rest of the Bible reveals His work to restore it.

Calling God Creator means our lives are received rather than self-created. The world matters because it belongs to Him, and people matter because each bears the dignity He gave.

Holy, just and compassionate

In Exodus 34, God describes Himself as compassionate and gracious, slow to anger, rich in faithful love, forgiving wrongdoing and committed to justice. These qualities belong together. God's mercy is not indifference to evil, and His justice is not lacking in compassion.

The Bible shows God listening to suffering, opposing oppression, forgiving those who turn to Him and remaining faithful even when people fail. At times His judgements can be difficult to understand, but they arise from His holiness and commitment to what is right.

The God who makes and keeps promises

God reveals Himself through action as well as description. He makes promises to Abraham and David, rescues Israel from slavery, speaks through the prophets and sends His Son. His faithfulness across generations gives the gospel its certainty.

Jesus teaches his followers to address God as Father. This does not make God small or familiar in a careless way. It expresses trust, dependence and the astonishing invitation to know the Creator personally. Jesus prayed to his Father, obeyed Him and made His character visible through a life of compassion, truth and faithful service.

The Bible presents God as

  • the one Creator and source of life;
  • holy, righteous and opposed to evil;
  • compassionate, patient and ready to forgive;
  • faithful to His promises;
  • the Father of Jesus Christ, inviting people to know Him.

Knowing God is more than agreeing that He exists. It involves learning to trust His character, listen to His word and share His values. The Bible's hope is that the earth will one day be filled with the knowledge of God, not merely awareness of His name but lives and societies shaped by His goodness.
