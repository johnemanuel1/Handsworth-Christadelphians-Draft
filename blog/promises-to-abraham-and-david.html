What Are the Promises to Abraham and David?

Suggested reading: Genesis 12:1–3 and 2 Samuel 7:8–16

The gospel did not begin in the New Testament. Long before Jesus was born, God made promises that established the shape of His purpose and created hope for the whole world.

Two people are especially important: Abraham, the ancestor of Israel, and David, Israel's most famous king. The New Testament opens by identifying Jesus as "the son of David, the son of Abraham" because he fulfils promises made to both.

Blessing promised through Abraham

God called Abraham to leave his homeland and trust Him. God promised Abraham a family, a land and a future through which all nations would be blessed. Later promises developed this hope, describing descendants as numerous as the stars and an inheritance secured by God's faithfulness.

Abraham died without seeing the complete fulfilment. His life therefore became an example of faith: he trusted what God had promised even while waiting. The New Testament explains that the promise points ultimately to Jesus and can include people from every nation who share Abraham's faith.

This means the gospel is not limited to one ethnic group. Through Jesus, people are invited into a promise of blessing, reconciliation and inheritance within God's renewed world.

A lasting king promised through David

Centuries later, God promised David that one of his descendants would establish an enduring throne and kingdom. Some aspects were reflected in David's son Solomon, but the language reaches beyond any ordinary king. Israel's prophets continued to look forward to a righteous ruler from David's family who would defend the poor, bring peace and rule the nations justly.

Jesus is repeatedly called Son of David. The angel told Mary that God would give him David's throne and that his kingdom would never end. Jesus' resurrection guarantees that this promised king lives and can return to reign.

Promises that hold the gospel together

The promises to Abraham and David connect subjects that can otherwise seem separate. They join Jesus with the Old Testament, personal salvation with the future of the earth, and faith today with God's coming Kingdom.

Through these promises, God offers

  • blessing that reaches people from every nation;
  • an inheritance in the land and renewed earth;
  • a faithful descendant through whom the promises are fulfilled;
  • a righteous king from David's family;
  • a Kingdom that will endure.

The promises also reveal God's character. Their fulfilment spans generations, showing that delay does not mean forgetfulness. God works patiently and remains faithful to His word.

Understanding these promises helps explain why Jesus preached the Kingdom, why his resurrection matters and why the Bible's hope concerns renewed life on earth. The gospel invites us to trust the same God Abraham trusted and to become connected with His promises through Jesus Christ.

Next step

Read Genesis 12:1–3 and 2 Samuel 7:8–16. List what God promises and notice which promises reach beyond Abraham or David personally.
