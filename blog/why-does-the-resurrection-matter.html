Why Does the Resurrection Matter?

Suggested reading: 1 Corinthians 15:1–26

The resurrection of Jesus is not an optional addition to Christianity. It confirms who Jesus is, demonstrates God's power over death and provides the foundation for the Bible's future hope.

The earliest Christians did not simply preserve the teaching of a dead leader. They announced that God had raised Jesus and that many witnesses had seen him alive.

It confirms Jesus' message

Jesus preached the Kingdom of God, called people to follow him and willingly gave his life. His crucifixion could have appeared to prove that his claims had failed. Instead, God raised him on the third day.

The resurrection is God's declaration that Jesus is His faithful Son and appointed king. It confirms that his sacrifice was accepted and that his teaching about the future can be trusted.

It is presented as a real event

In 1 Corinthians 15, Paul summarises the message he had received and passed on: Jesus died, was buried, was raised and appeared to witnesses. He names individuals, the apostles and a gathering of more than five hundred people.

Paul's point is that the resurrection was not originally presented as a private vision or a legend developed much later. It was a public claim at the heart of the first Christian preaching.

Evidence alone cannot force faith, and questions deserve careful exploration. Yet the transformation of Jesus' frightened followers and the rapid growth of their resurrection message require an explanation.

It gives hope beyond death

Paul calls Jesus the "firstfruits" of those who have died. The firstfruits are the beginning of a harvest and the assurance that more will follow. Jesus was raised first; those who belong to him will be raised when he returns.

This is more than the idea that life somehow continues. The Bible teaches that God can restore the dead to embodied, meaningful life. Jesus' resurrection guarantees that death will not have the final word.

Because Jesus was raised

  • his identity and message are confirmed;
  • his followers have reason to trust God's promises;
  • forgiveness and a new life are possible;
  • future resurrection is more than wishful thinking;
  • Jesus lives and can return as king.

It changes life now

If death is final, even our best efforts can seem temporary. Resurrection gives present choices lasting significance. Paul concludes that faithful work is not in vain because God's future is real.

Acts of love, courage and faithfulness matter. Believers are invited to live now as people shaped by the life of the risen Jesus and confident in the Kingdom he will establish.

The resurrection therefore turns the cross from apparent defeat into victory and points forward to the next great foundation of the gospel: the Kingdom of God.
