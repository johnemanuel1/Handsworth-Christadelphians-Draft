What Is Bible Prophecy For?

Suggested reading: Isaiah 46:8–13

Bible prophecy is sometimes treated like a puzzle for predicting headlines. In Scripture, however, prophecy has a much richer purpose: it reveals God, calls people to change and gives trustworthy hope.

A prophet was primarily someone who spoke God's message. Sometimes that message described future events, but prophets also confronted wrongdoing, encouraged faithfulness and explained what God was doing in their own time. Their words joined warning with invitation.

Revealing the God who acts

In Isaiah 46, God contrasts Himself with powerless idols. He declares the end from the beginning and promises to accomplish His purpose. Prophecy presents history as meaningful rather than directionless. God knows where His creation is going and is able to fulfil what He has promised.

Fulfilled prophecy gives reasons for confidence. The Old Testament anticipated the rise and fall of kingdoms, the consequences of Israel's unfaithfulness and important features of the Messiah's work. Jesus and the apostles repeatedly pointed to these Scriptures to explain his life, death and resurrection.

Warning and calling people back

Prophecy was never given merely to satisfy curiosity. When prophets warned of judgment, they usually called people to repent. Their message was practical: injustice mattered, worship without integrity was empty, and trusting human power instead of God would fail.

This remains relevant. Prophecy asks whether our lives are aligned with God's values. Knowledge about the future is of little value if it produces pride, fear or endless speculation rather than faith, humility and changed behaviour.

Giving hope

Many prophecies look beyond immediate trouble to God's promised Kingdom. Isaiah describes peace among nations and a righteous ruler. Daniel sees human empires replaced by a Kingdom established by God. The prophets speak of resurrection, restoration and the earth filled with God's glory.

Christadelphians believe these promises will be fulfilled through the return of Jesus to the earth. We also believe caution is essential. Jesus said nobody knows the day or hour of his return. Attempts to set dates or force every news event into a prophetic scheme can distract from the clear message: God will act, Jesus will return, and his followers should be ready.

Good prophecy reading asks

  • What did this message mean to its first hearers?
  • What does it reveal about God's character and purpose?
  • Has any part already been fulfilled?
  • How does it connect with Jesus and God's Kingdom?
  • What faithful response does it encourage?

Prophecy gives a long view of God's work. It reminds us that today's powers and troubles are not permanent, while God's purpose is. That confidence should lead to patient hope and faithful living rather than anxiety.
