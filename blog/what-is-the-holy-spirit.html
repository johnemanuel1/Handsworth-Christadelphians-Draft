What Is the Holy Spirit?

Suggested reading: Luke 1:26–38

The Holy Spirit is central to the Bible's account of creation, revelation and salvation. But what does Scripture mean when it speaks about God's Spirit?

The words translated spirit in the Bible can also mean breath or wind. They describe an unseen power known by its effects. When the Bible speaks of God's Spirit, it describes God reaching into His creation and accomplishing His will.

God's power in action

At creation, God's Spirit moved over the waters. The Psalms describe living creatures receiving life through God's Spirit. Prophets spoke as they were moved by the Holy Spirit, and remarkable works were accomplished through God's power.

The word holy means set apart for God. The Holy Spirit is therefore not ordinary human energy or vague spirituality. It is God's own power and activity, directed according to His character and purpose.

The Father, the Son and the Spirit

Christadelphians believe the Bible teaches that God is one, the Father. We understand the Holy Spirit not as a separate divine person, but as God's Spirit: His power, presence and influence at work.

Luke's account of Jesus' birth helps explain this. The angel tells Mary that the Holy Spirit will come upon her and that the power of the Most High will overshadow her. The parallel wording connects the Holy Spirit directly with God's power. Through this action, Jesus is born as God's Son.

Jesus was given the Spirit without measure. God's power enabled his miracles, while his words perfectly revealed the Father's mind. After Jesus' resurrection, the apostles received the Spirit to witness boldly and confirm the gospel message.

God's Spirit and believers

The New Testament also speaks about lives shaped by the Spirit. God's word, described as inspired or "God-breathed", reveals His thinking. When believers listen to that word and allow it to change them, they begin to develop what Paul calls the fruit of the Spirit: love, joy, peace, patience, kindness and self-control.

This does not mean personal growth happens through human effort alone. God works through His word, providence, prayer and community to shape people who trust Him. Yet claims about the Spirit should be tested carefully by Scripture and by the character they produce.

The Holy Spirit is connected with

  • God's work in creation and the gift of life;
  • the inspiration of prophets and Scripture;
  • the birth, teaching and miracles of Jesus;
  • the apostles' witness to the risen Christ;
  • lives increasingly shaped by God's character.

The Bible's teaching about the Spirit directs attention toward God and His work rather than toward human excitement. It invites us to recognise God's power, listen to His revealed word and become people who reflect His ways.
