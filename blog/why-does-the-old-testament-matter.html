Why Does the Old Testament Matter?

Suggested reading: Psalm 72

New readers often begin with Jesus in the Gospels, which is an excellent starting point. Yet understanding Jesus fully soon leads us back to the Old Testament.

The Old Testament makes up most of the Bible. It contains unfamiliar customs, long histories and sometimes difficult passages, so it can feel distant. However, it introduces the God Jesus worshipped, establishes the promises he fulfilled and provides the language he used to explain his mission.

It introduces God's character

The Old Testament presents God as Creator, judge, rescuer, king and compassionate Father. It shows His holiness and justice, but also His patience, mercy and faithfulness. When Jesus speaks about loving God and neighbour, forgiveness, justice or the Kingdom, he builds upon this earlier revelation.

Its stories are honest about humanity too. Individuals and nations repeatedly struggle with pride, fear, injustice and faithlessness. Their experiences help us recognise our own need for forgiveness and change.

It contains the promises behind the gospel

God's promises to Abraham describe blessing reaching every nation. His promise to David looks forward to a righteous king whose rule will endure. The prophets describe a restored world, resurrection from death, forgiven people and nations learning peace.

These promises explain why the New Testament calls Jesus the Christ, Son of David and descendant of Abraham. Without the Old Testament, those titles lose much of their meaning. The gospel is not a new idea appearing suddenly; it is the fulfilment of a purpose patiently revealed across centuries.

It was the Bible of Jesus and the apostles

Jesus quoted the Old Testament, used it when facing temptation and explained his work from it. The apostles used the same Scriptures when preaching the resurrection and Kingdom of God. When New Testament writers refer to "the Scriptures", they frequently mean what we call the Old Testament.

Psalm 72 offers a good example. It describes an ideal king who judges with righteousness, defends the needy, brings peace and receives honour from all nations. The psalm reaches beyond any ordinary ruler and helps us understand the Bible's hope for the reign of Jesus.

How to begin exploring it

  • Read Genesis to discover creation and the foundational promises.
  • Read selected psalms to hear faith expressed in worship and struggle.
  • Read about Israel's history while noticing both God's faithfulness and human failure.
  • Read prophets such as Isaiah to explore the hope of God's Kingdom.
  • Notice Old Testament quotations when reading the New Testament.

Christians are not called to copy every ancient law or custom. We first ask what a passage meant in its setting and then how it contributes to God's revealed purpose. Read in that way, the Old Testament becomes more than background information. It deepens our understanding of God, gives substance to the gospel and enlarges our hope.

Reaching the end of this beginner series is therefore not the end of the journey. It is an invitation to return to the beginning and discover how much more the Bible has to reveal.
