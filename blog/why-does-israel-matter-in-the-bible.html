Why Does Israel Matter in the Bible?

Suggested reading: Romans 11:1–29

Israel appears throughout the Bible, from Genesis to Revelation. Understanding why helps us see the faithfulness of God and the shape of His purpose for the whole world.

The Bible's interest in Israel does not begin with the modern state or with the idea that one nation is naturally better than others. It begins with God's promises. God called Abraham and promised that through his family every nation would be blessed. Abraham's grandson Jacob was later given the name Israel, and his descendants became the people of Israel.

Chosen for a purpose

God rescued Israel from slavery in Egypt and invited them into a covenant relationship. They were called to know God, live by His ways and become a light to surrounding nations. Through them, God preserved His word, revealed His character and prepared the way for the promised Messiah.

Being chosen did not mean Israel was always faithful or exempt from responsibility. Much of the Old Testament records the nation's failures alongside God's patience. The prophets challenged injustice and idolatry, warned of judgment and also promised restoration. Israel's story therefore shows both the seriousness of sin and the dependability of God.

Jesus and Israel's story

Jesus was born into the people of Israel. He was descended from Abraham and David, worshipped the God of Israel and fulfilled promises made through Israel's Scriptures. His first followers were Jewish, and the gospel they preached announced that God's promised blessing was now being opened to all nations through Jesus.

Non-Jewish believers do not replace Israel or become superior to Jewish people. Paul describes them as branches grafted into an existing olive tree. They share in God's promises by faith and are warned against pride. The same God remains faithful to His purpose and calls every person to trust Him.

Israel and the future

The survival and return of the Jewish people after centuries of dispersion is remarkable. Christadelphians see it as consistent with Bible prophecies that speak of Israel's regathering before a final spiritual restoration. This does not mean approving every decision made by a government or ignoring the suffering of Palestinians and others. Biblical hope includes justice, mercy and peace for all peoples.

Ultimately, Israel matters because God's promises matter. The Kingdom of God will be ruled by Jesus, Israel's Messiah, and will bring blessing to every nation. The Bible's final vision is not one people triumphing over others, but a world united in the knowledge and worship of God.

Israel helps us understand

  • God's promises to Abraham and David;
  • the setting and identity of Jesus;
  • God's patience, justice and faithfulness;
  • the gospel's invitation to every nation;
  • the Bible's hope for worldwide peace.
