Who Is Jesus Christ?

Suggested reading: Luke 1:26–38

Jesus stands at the centre of the gospel. To understand his message, death, resurrection and future work, we first need to listen carefully to how the Bible introduces him.

The name Jesus means "God saves". The title Christ means "anointed one" and is the Greek equivalent of Messiah. Calling him Jesus Christ therefore identifies him as the person chosen by God to bring salvation and fulfil the promises of Scripture.

God's Son and a real human being

Luke records that Jesus was born to Mary through God's power. The angel explains that for this reason he would be called the Son of God. Jesus did not have a human father, yet he was genuinely born, grew, experienced weakness, faced temptation and died.

Christadelphians understand Jesus to be God's unique Son, not God the Son. Jesus repeatedly distinguishes himself from his Father, prays to God and describes the Father as greater than himself. At the same time, he reveals God's character more perfectly than anyone else. To hear Jesus is to hear the Father's message; to see his compassion, holiness and truth is to see what God is like.

Both truths matter. Because Jesus is God's Son, his life and mission come uniquely from the Father. Because he truly shares our humanity, he can understand human weakness, represent us faithfully and show that obedience to God is possible even under the greatest pressure.

The promised Messiah

Jesus belongs to the Bible's developing story. He is descended from Abraham and David and fulfils promises made long before his birth. As Son of David, he is the promised king. As Abraham's descendant, he is the one through whom blessing reaches the nations.

His teaching focused on the Kingdom of God. His miracles were signs of God's restoring power: the sick healed, the excluded welcomed and even the dead raised. These acts offered glimpses of the greater renewal his future reign will bring.

Faithful where humanity failed

Jesus shared human nature and faced genuine temptation, yet remained obedient to God. He loved completely, spoke truthfully and trusted his Father even when faithfulness led to the cross. Through him, God shows both the life humanity was intended to live and the way sin and death will be overcome.

God raised Jesus from the dead and gave him immortality. The risen Jesus is now at God's right hand and will return to establish the Kingdom he preached.

The Bible presents Jesus as

  • the unique Son of God, born to Mary;
  • a real human being who faced temptation;
  • the promised Messiah and descendant of David;
  • the faithful servant who died and was raised;
  • the future king who will return.

Jesus invites more than admiration. He calls people to trust his message, receive forgiveness and follow him. The next articles explore the promises he fulfils and why his death became central to the gospel.
