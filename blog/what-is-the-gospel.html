What Is the Gospel?

Suggested reading: Acts 8:5–12

The word gospel means good news. In the Bible, it is the good news of what God has done through Jesus Christ and what He has promised to do for the world.

Sometimes the gospel is reduced to a general message that God loves us or that people can go to heaven. Those ideas may sound familiar, but the Bible gives the gospel a richer and more definite shape.

Good news about a Kingdom

Jesus travelled through towns and villages preaching "the gospel of the kingdom of God". He announced that God's promised rule was drawing near and called people to believe and change direction. The Kingdom was not merely a feeling in people's hearts. It was the hope of God's righteous rule transforming the earth.

This hope had deep roots. The Old Testament promised blessing for all nations, a just king descended from David, peace, resurrection and a world filled with the knowledge of God. Jesus taught that he was central to the fulfilment of those promises.

Good news about Jesus

The gospel is therefore also about Jesus himself. He is God's Son and the promised Christ. Through his faithful life, sacrificial death and resurrection, God has opened the way to forgiveness, reconciliation and eternal life.

Jesus' resurrection is essential to the good news. It demonstrates that death can be overcome and confirms that Jesus will return. Christian hope is not based only on what happened in the past, but on what the risen Jesus will do in the future.

Acts 8 gives a helpful summary. Philip went to Samaria and preached "the good news of the kingdom of God and the name of Jesus Christ". His message held these two parts together: God's coming Kingdom and the saving work of its king.

The gospel announces

  • God is the Creator and has not abandoned His purpose for the earth.
  • Jesus is God's Son, the promised Christ and future king.
  • Jesus died for sins and God raised him from the dead.
  • Jesus will return to establish God's Kingdom.
  • God invites people to believe, repent, be baptised and follow Jesus.

Good news that invites a response

The gospel is a gift before it is a task. It announces God's grace, promises and action. Yet good news this significant naturally asks for a response. Jesus called people to repent, meaning to reconsider their direction and turn toward God. The apostles invited believers to be baptised and begin a new life of faith.

Exploring the gospel is therefore more than learning religious facts. It is discovering a purpose large enough to include the future of the world and personal enough to change how we live today.

The remaining articles in this pathway explore each foundation more closely: who God is, why Jesus matters, what God has promised, and how people can respond.
