Where Should I Start Reading the Bible?

Suggested reading: Luke 1

Opening the Bible for the first time can feel overwhelming. It contains 66 books, spans thousands of years of history, and includes everything from poetry and prophecy to letters and biographies. So where should you begin?

A good place to start is the Gospel of Luke.

Luke was a careful historian who set out to record the life of Jesus Christ in an orderly way. Right from the opening chapter, we are introduced to ordinary people whose lives were changed by God's promises and His purpose.

Many people assume the Bible is mainly a collection of rules or ancient stories. In reality, it tells one great story: God's plan to bring hope, forgiveness, and eternal life to the world through Jesus Christ.

Luke helps us discover that story through the eyes of people who met Jesus, listened to his teaching, witnessed his miracles, and saw the impact he had on their lives.

Many people come to the Bible looking for answers to life's biggest questions.

Why are we here? Does God exist? Is there a purpose behind the struggles and joys of life? What happens in the future? Can people really change?

The Bible does not ignore these questions. In fact, they are woven throughout its pages. It introduces us to real people who experienced fear, doubt, failure, hope, loss, and joy—many of the same experiences we face today.

That is one reason the life of Jesus is such a good place to begin. As you read Luke's Gospel, you will encounter people from every walk of life: fishermen, tax collectors, religious leaders, the sick, the poor, and those searching for meaning. Again and again, Jesus meets people where they are and offers them hope, purpose, and a new direction.

You do not need to have all the answers before opening the Bible. Reading it is not about becoming an expert overnight. It is about beginning a journey of discovery and allowing God's message to speak for itself.

Questions to ask as you read Luke 1

  • What does this chapter teach me about God?
  • What does it tell me about Jesus?
  • What stands out or surprises me?
  • What questions would I like answered?

Don't worry if you don't understand everything straight away. The Bible is a book that rewards patient reading and reflection. Even lifelong readers continue to discover new insights.

A helpful approach is to read a small section each day. Ten minutes is enough to begin building a habit. Keep a notebook nearby and write down anything that interests, encourages, or puzzles you.

Most importantly, read with an open mind. The Bible claims to be God's message to humanity, and millions of people have found hope, purpose, and direction through its pages.

If you're just starting your Bible journey, Luke's Gospel is an excellent place to begin.

Why not read Luke chapter 1 today and discover the story for yourself?
