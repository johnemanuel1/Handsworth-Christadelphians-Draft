What Does the Bible Teach About Angels?

Suggested reading: Psalm 103:19–22

Angels appear at important moments throughout the Bible. They announce, protect, guide and carry out God's commands, always directing attention beyond themselves to the God they serve.

The word angel means messenger. Sometimes the original Bible words can describe human messengers, but they frequently refer to powerful heavenly beings sent by God.

God's faithful servants

Psalm 103 describes angels as mighty ones who obey God's word and do His will. They are not rival gods or independent powers. They serve the one Creator and participate in His purpose.

Angels announced the birth of Jesus, strengthened him during suffering and declared his resurrection. In the Old Testament, they guided Abraham's family, protected God's people and delivered messages to prophets. Their appearances often caused fear, yet their repeated words were, "Do not be afraid."

Christadelphians understand angels to be immortal beings who do not sin. Jesus said that angels in heaven always behold the Father's face, and he connected resurrection life with becoming like the angels, no longer subject to death.

Angels and God's presence

At times, an angel speaks with God's authority and is addressed as though God Himself were present. This reflects representation: the messenger carries the name and authority of the sender. It does not mean each angel is God.

This principle helps explain several Old Testament passages where "the angel of the Lord" speaks in the first person on God's behalf. The encounter is genuinely with God's message and authority, delivered through His servant.

Do angels help people today?

Hebrews describes angels as ministering spirits sent to serve those who will inherit salvation. The Bible gives examples of angelic protection, sometimes visible and sometimes unknown to the people helped. This gives confidence that God's care can extend far beyond what we see.

However, Scripture does not encourage people to pray to angels, seek contact with them or build detailed theories about unseen activity. Worship belongs to God. Our confidence rests in His care, whether He acts through angels, ordinary people or circumstances we never fully understand.

Popular images can also distract from the Bible's description. Angels are not presented as deceased humans who have gained wings. Human hope is resurrection, while angels are a distinct part of God's creation.

The Bible presents angels as

  • God's powerful and obedient servants;
  • messengers who speak with His authority;
  • participants in key moments of His purpose;
  • ministers involved in God's care;
  • a picture of immortal life beyond death.

Angels remind us that reality is larger than what we can see, yet they keep the focus firmly on God. They rejoice in His saving work and faithfully carry out His will.

The final four articles turn from understanding Bible language to the human need for salvation and the life God invites us to share.
